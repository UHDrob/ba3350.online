Methodology

The research was executed based on a rapid systematic review approach. The researcher assessed what was previously known regarding the question of whether MNC employees should get CCC training through the utilization of a systematic review approach to search, as well as judgmentally appraise, the then-available literature. Notably, rapid reviews are as well christened rapid evidence assessments, rapid evidence summaries, expedited reviews, rapid evidence reviews, and rapid systematic reviews (Dobbins 4-7). The researcher synthesized information or evidence from different sources, providing rather timely findings related to the question. Rapid reviews hasten processes of systematic reviews through the omission of some systematic review stages – that elementarily portends that rapid reviews are not as rigorous as the typical systematic reviews. In the research, the researcher followed various steps. First, the researcher planned the review by firming up the research question that was to form the foundation of the research - the question of whether MNC employees should get CCC training. Second, the researcher looked for relevant literature from different sources, including particular books and websites. Third, the researcher analyzed the literature – results. Fourth, the researcher synthesized or assessed findings from the different sources that were considered in the research.
